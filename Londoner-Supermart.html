Project Overview

This project presents a comprehensive sales analysis for Londoner SuperMart, leveraging Excel to transform raw transactional data into actionable insights. The dataset includes over 9,000 orders spanning multiple years, customer segments, product categories, and geographic regions.

The dashboard enables stakeholders to monitor performance, identify trends, and make data-driven decisions through interactive filters and dynamic visualizations.

Key Business Insights

Seasonal Trends

Sales and orders peak in November and December, indicating strong holiday season performance with steady growth from Q1 to Q4 across most years.

Weekly Patterns

Fridays and Mondays show the highest sales volumes, suggesting strategic promotional opportunities, while Sundays consistently record the lowest activity.

Top Products

Phones lead revenue ($330,007), followed by Chairs ($218,965) and Storage ($114,747), highlighting key product categories driving business growth.

Geographic Performance

California dominates with $457,687 in sales, followed by New York ($310,876) and Washington ($138,441), revealing regional market strengths.

Technical Implementation

Data Processing & Analysis

  • Data Cleaning: Standardized formats, handled missing values, and validated data integrity
  • Pivot Tables: Dynamic aggregation of sales data by multiple dimensions
  • Chart Integration: Dual-axis charts comparing orders vs sales trends
  • Interactive Elements: Slicers for real-time data filtering and exploration

Dashboard Features

  • Top summary cards with key metrics
  • Monthly & daily sales analysis with trend visualization
  • Product sub-category performance rankings
  • Geographic sales distribution by state
  • Customer segment analysis (Consumer, Corporate, Home Office)
